# **MOTION AND INTERACTION SYSTEM.md**

## **GARYMARINER.COM — MOTION & INTERACTION SYSTEM V1**

---

# **CORE MOTION THESIS**

Motion on GaryMariner.com must feel like:

intelligent infrastructure quietly responding to human presence.

Motion should communicate:

* executive calm  
* technological sophistication  
* systems intelligence  
* environmental awareness  
* cinematic restraint

Motion must never feel:

* playful  
* gimmicky  
* chaotic  
* hyperactive  
* trendy  
* decorative  
* “AI demo reel”

The goal is not to impress through movement.

The goal is to create:

# **presence.**

---

# **MOTION PRINCIPLES**

## **1\. Motion Must Serve Psychology**

Every movement must support one of these outcomes:

* intrigue  
* clarity  
* authority  
* immersion  
* progression  
* trust  
* ecosystem gravity

If motion does not improve the user’s emotional or intellectual journey, remove it.

---

## **2\. Slow Is Powerful**

Fast motion often feels cheap.

This site should move with:

* intention  
* patience  
* confidence  
* control

Motion should feel like:  
systems waking up,  
not content flying in.

---

## **3\. The Environment Moves Before The Interface**

The background, lighting, atmosphere, and spatial layers should create life first.

UI elements should move second.

This creates immersion without feeling like a template.

---

## **4\. Motion Should Reveal Intelligence**

Interactions should imply:

* alignment  
* translation  
* structure  
* responsiveness  
* hidden systems

The user should feel:

“The interface understands where I am.”

---

## **5\. Restraint Is Mandatory**

No element should animate just because it can.

Avoid:

* excessive hover effects  
* bouncing elements  
* constant loops  
* overdone parallax  
* aggressive scroll-jacking  
* animation clutter

Luxury is selective.

---

# **MOTION HIERARCHY**

Motion should exist in four layers.

---

## **LAYER 1 — ENVIRONMENTAL MOTION**

Highest priority.

This is the ambient world of the site.

Used for:

* atmospheric lighting  
* subtle gradients  
* dimensional depth  
* infrastructure movement  
* slow background shifts  
* spatial breathing

Behavior:

* slow  
* nearly invisible  
* continuous or scroll-reactive  
* calm

Purpose:  
to make the site feel alive.

---

## **LAYER 2 — STRUCTURAL MOTION**

Used to transition between scenes.

This includes:

* scene reveals  
* section progression  
* layout shifts  
* depth changes  
* environmental transitions

Behavior:

* cinematic  
* directed  
* smooth  
* intentional

Purpose:  
to make the homepage feel authored, not stacked.

---

## **LAYER 3 — CONTENT MOTION**

Used for:

* typography reveals  
* image reveals  
* content sequencing  
* node activation  
* ecosystem routing moments

Behavior:

* restrained  
* staggered  
* rhythm-based  
* editorial

Purpose:  
to control reading pace and attention.

---

## **LAYER 4 — MICRO-INTERACTIONS**

Used for:

* buttons  
* links  
* cards  
* nav  
* ecosystem nodes  
* media modules

Behavior:

* subtle  
* quick  
* tactile  
* premium

Purpose:  
to reward interaction without distracting.

---

the environment coming online.

Use:

* slow ambient lighting shifts

* controlled typography emergence  
* atmospheric particle/noise movement  
* soft spatial reveals

Avoid:

* hard cuts  
* fast hero animation  
* excessive text movement  
* generic typewriter effects

The scene should feel:  
alive before it explains.

## **SCENE 2 — THE TRANSLATOR**

Motion should become:  
more structured.

Use:

* alignment animations  
* connecting line movement  
* layered systems forming  
* subtle node-to-node flow  
* copy appearing in intentional sequence

The motion should imply:  
complexity becoming clear.

---

## **SCENE 3 — SYSTEMS & INFRASTRUCTURE**

Motion should feel:  
operational.

Use:

* ambient interface layering  
* depth shifts  
* modular information reveals  
* infrastructure pattern movement  
* slow systems activation

Avoid:  
fake dashboards,  
random data animations,  
generic tech UI noise.

The motion should imply:  
systems running beneath the surface.

---

## **SCENE 4 — HUMAN-CENTERED TRANSFORMATION**

Motion should slow.

Use:

* breathing-like atmospheric movement

The scene should feel:  
human,  
grounded,  
reflective.

---

## **SCENE 5 — THE ECOSYSTEM**

Motion should imply:  
network intelligence.

Use:

* ecosystem node activation  
* spatial relationship mapping  
* soft pathway reveals  
* connected initiative transitions  
* directional hover states

Avoid:  
logo walls,  
spinning networks,  
overcomplicated constellation graphics.

The ecosystem should feel:  
curated,  
not chaotic.

---

## **SCENE 6 — CONTROLLED INVITATION**

Motion should resolve.

Use:

* slow fade-in  
* quiet environmental settling  
* minimal CTA interaction  
* reduced background movement

The ending should feel:  
calm,  
intentional,  
open.

---

# **INTERACTION PHILOSOPHY**

Interactions should feel:  
premium,  
intelligent,  
and inevitable.

The user should never feel like they are playing with a UI toy.

They should feel like they are moving through:  
a strategic environment.

---

# **HOVER STATES**

Hover states should:

* reveal depth  
* clarify direction  
* signal interactivity  
* enhance perceived quality

Avoid:

* exaggerated scaling  
* glowing everything  
* noisy color shifts  
* playful bounces

Recommended hover behavior:

* slight lift  
* subtle light sweep  
* border illumination  
* background depth shift  
* micro-copy reveal  
* directional arrow motion

---

# **BUTTON INTERACTIONS**

Buttons should feel:  
executive and restrained.

Primary button behavior:

* subtle background shift  
* thin border illumination  
* slight translate  
* arrow movement  
* soft glow only when appropriate

Avoid:

* oversized glow  
* bounce  
* pulse spam  
* aggressive gradients

CTA buttons should feel:  
like invitations,  
not advertisements.

---

# **NAVIGATION INTERACTIONS**

Navigation should feel:  
quiet but intelligent.

Recommended:

* minimal top nav  
* sticky only if elegant  
* subtle active-state indicators  
* soft blur/glass if used carefully  
* scroll-aware nav compression

Avoid:

* bulky nav bars  
* too many links  
* dropdown clutter  
* social icon noise

Navigation should support:  
ecosystem movement,  
not dominate the experience.

---

# **SCROLL BEHAVIOR**

Scrolling should feel:  
cinematic but not restrictive.

Use:

* natural scroll  
* scroll-triggered reveals  
* scene-aware transitions  
* subtle parallax  
* depth progression

Avoid:

* heavy scroll-jacking  
* forced snapping everywhere  
* scroll fatigue  
* motion that blocks reading

Scroll should feel:  
directed,  
not controlled.

---

# **PARALLAX RULES**

Parallax may be used,  
but only with restraint.

Good parallax:

* creates depth  
* supports atmosphere  
* enhances scene transition

Bad parallax:

* makes the site feel gimmicky  
* distracts from reading  
* causes performance problems

Use parallax on:

* environmental layers  
* hero atmosphere  
* background infrastructure  
* ecosystem depth

Avoid parallax on:

* large blocks of text  
* critical CTAs  
* form elements  
* primary reading content

---

# **TYPOGRAPHY MOTION**

Typography should reveal like:  
an editorial thought entering the room.

Recommended:

* opacity \+ slight vertical movement  
* staggered line reveals  
* masking reveals  
* slow confidence-based entrance

Avoid:

* typewriter effects  
* bouncing letters  
* random word animations  
* overly dramatic split text everywhere

Text should feel:  
composed,  
not theatrical.

---

# **IMAGE MOTION**

Images should feel:  
cinematic and intentional.

Recommended:

* soft mask reveals  
* slow scale-in  
* subtle parallax depth  
* atmospheric overlay transitions  
* directional lighting shifts

Avoid:

* spinning  
* flipping  
* excessive zoom  
* carousel fatigue

Images should feel:  
selected,  
not inserted.

---

# **ECOSYSTEM NODE INTERACTIONS**

Ecosystem nodes should represent:  
living initiatives.

Each node should:

* reveal context on hover/tap  
* show directional pathway  
* connect to the larger ecosystem  
* avoid looking like a logo grid

Possible node categories:

* Mariner Nexus  
* Undugu  
* media/podcast  
* strategic advisory  
* thought leadership  
* collaboration

Interactions should feel:  
like entering deeper layers of a network.

---

# **MEDIA INTERACTION SYSTEM**

Media should enhance authority,  
not turn the site into a content feed.

Podcast/media interactions should:

* preview context  
* show intellectual range  
* allow deeper exploration  
* remain visually premium

Avoid:

* messy embedded widgets  
* autoplay audio  
* cluttered episode grids  
* social feed dumping

Media should feel:  
curated,  
editorial,  
selective.

---

# **FORM / CONTACT INTERACTIONS**

Contact should feel:  
selective and executive.

Forms should be:

* minimal  
* direct  
* intelligent  
* low-friction

Avoid:

* long generic forms  
* “tell us everything” intake forms  
* desperate sales language

Preferred language:

* Start a conversation  
* Explore alignment  
* Open a strategic dialogue  
* Build what comes next

---

# **PERFORMANCE RULES**

Motion cannot sacrifice performance.

Mandatory:

* optimized animation libraries  
* GPU-friendly transforms  
* no layout thrashing  
* reduced-motion support  
* lazy-load heavy visuals  
* avoid excessive client-side animation  
* mobile-first performance testing

Preferred animation stack:

* Framer Motion for component-level choreography  
* GSAP only for advanced scroll/cinematic sequences  
* CSS transitions for simple micro-interactions  
* WebGL/WebGPU only if it serves the experience and stays performant

No pesado, Papi. No bloated nonsense.

---

# **ACCESSIBILITY RULES**

Motion must respect user needs.

Required:

* prefers-reduced-motion support  
* readable text at all times  
* no essential information hidden behind hover only  
* no seizure-risk flashing  
* no excessive movement around body copy  
* touch-friendly mobile interactions

Reduced-motion mode should:  
preserve elegance,  
not remove the experience entirely.

---

# **MOBILE INTERACTION RULES**

Mobile should feel:  
immersive,  
not compromised.

Mobile motion must be:

* lighter  
* faster to render  
* easier to read  
* thumb-friendly  
* less layered than desktop

Avoid:

* heavy parallax  
* complex hover-dependent behavior  
* desktop animation transplanted directly  
* giant scroll delays

Mobile should keep:

* cinematic pacing  
* atmospheric depth  
* editorial hierarchy  
* controlled transitions

---

8–30 seconds

ambient atmosphere.

700ms–1400ms

Purpose:  
cinematic progression.

---

## **Content Reveals**

Duration:  
500ms–900ms

Purpose:  
reading rhythm.

---

## **Micro-Interactions**

Duration:  
150ms–300ms

Purpose:  
responsiveness.

---

## **CTA Interactions**

Duration:  
200ms–400ms

Purpose:  
premium tactile response.

---

# **EASING PHILOSOPHY**

Motion should feel:  
natural,  
weighted,  
and elegant.

Use:

* ease-out for reveals  
* ease-in-out for scene transitions  
* spring only when subtle  
* cubic-bezier curves for cinematic motion

Avoid:

* elastic bounces  
* harsh linear movement  
* cartoon physics  
* overly playful springs

---

# **ABSOLUTE MOTION BANS**

Do not use:

* typewriter hero text  
* bouncing icons  
* spinning logos  
* constant pulsing CTAs  
* excessive glow loops  
* random particles everywhere  
* heavy scroll hijacking  
* noisy cursor trails  
* fake hacker/code rain aesthetics  
* generic AI neural network animations

These weaken authority.

Straight to the trash.

---

# **FINAL MOTION STANDARD**

Motion is successful only if the visitor feels:

“This environment is intelligent, composed, and alive.”

Not:

“This website has cool animations.”

That difference is the standard.
