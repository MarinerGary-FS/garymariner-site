# Design System — GaryMariner.com

## Design Objective

Create a premium, modern, executive-level visual system that reflects:

- strategic intelligence
- operational clarity
- high-end consulting presence
- controlled, intentional design

This site should feel closer to a **private equity firm or high-end consultancy** than a startup or freelancer portfolio.

---

## Core Design Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Decoration
Every element must serve a purpose.
No unnecessary visuals, no clutter.

---

### 2. Structure Over Flash
The layout should feel disciplined, aligned, and intentional.

---

### 3. Premium Restraint
Avoid overdesign.
Use minimal elements with high impact.

---

### 4. Typography Drives Authority
Strong typography is more important than graphics.

---

### 5. Consistency Builds Trust
Spacing, sizing, and alignment must feel consistent across the entire site.

---

## Color System

### Primary Colors
- Background: Deep Black / Charcoal (#0A0A0A or similar)
- Surface: Slightly lighter dark tones (#111 / #1A1A1A)

### Text Colors
- Primary Text: White (#FFFFFF)
- Secondary Text: Light Gray (#A0A0A0 - #C0C0C0)

### Accent Colors (use sparingly)
- Red (for power and intensity)
- Gold (for premium / legacy feel)

⚠️ Accent colors should never dominate the UI.

---

## Typography System

### Headings
- Bold, modern sans-serif
- Strong weight contrast (700–900)
- Large size for hierarchy

### Body Text
- Clean, readable sans-serif
- Medium weight (400–500)
- High line height for readability

### Typography Rules
- Clear hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- Avoid long paragraphs
- Use spacing to separate ideas
- Headlines should feel decisive

---

## Spacing System

### Section Spacing
- 80px – 120px vertical padding

### Internal Spacing
- 24px – 48px between elements

### Rules
- Never crowd sections
- Let content breathe
- Maintain consistent spacing rhythm

---

## Layout System

### Grid
- Clean, consistent grid
- Max width: ~1200px–1400px
- Centered content

### Structure
- Clear section boundaries
- Predictable layout flow
- Strong alignment

---

## Component System

### Buttons
- High contrast
- Rounded or slightly softened edges
- Clear hover states
- No overly flashy effects

### Cards
- Subtle background contrast
- Light border or soft shadow
- Consistent padding
- Used for services and case studies

### Sections
- Clear visual separation
- Alternating background tones (subtle)
- Strong spacing

---

## Motion & Interaction

### Motion Principles
- Subtle
- Purpose-driven
- Smooth

### Allowed Motion
- Fade-in on scroll
- Slight hover elevation
- Micro-interactions

### Avoid
- Heavy animations
- Distracting transitions
- Overuse of motion

---

## Imagery & Visual Assets

### Style
- Minimal
- High-quality
- Intentional

### Options
- Professional headshots
- Abstract tech visuals
- Subtle gradients or lighting effects

### Avoid
- Stock photo overload
- Cartoonish graphics
- Generic SaaS illustrations

---

## Iconography

- Simple and minimal
- Line-based icons preferred
- Used sparingly

---

## Visual Hierarchy Rules

1. Headline must dominate first view
2. Supporting text must be clearly secondary
3. CTA must stand out
4. Sections must feel distinct
5. Important numbers (proof) must be highly visible

---

## Dark Mode Philosophy

This is a **dark-first design**.

Reasons:
- feels premium
- reduces visual noise
- improves focus
- aligns with modern executive brands

---

## Responsive Design

### Mobile Rules
- Stack sections cleanly
- Reduce text length
- Maintain spacing integrity
- Keep CTAs visible and accessible

---

## What This Design Should Feel Like

- Premium
- Controlled
- Strategic
- High-end consulting
- Confident and intentional

---

## What This Design Should NOT Feel Like

- Startup template
- Freelancer portfolio
- Overdesigned UI
- Trendy for the sake of trends
- Cluttered or noisy

---

## Final Design Standard

If a section feels:
- generic
- busy
- unclear
- or unnecessary

It should be simplified or removed.

The goal is not to impress with design.

The goal is to communicate authority through clarity and structure.
